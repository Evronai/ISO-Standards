What is ISO?

The International Organization for Standardization (ISO) is an independent, non-governmental international organization that develops and publishes voluntary consensus-based international standards.[citation:1]

Who Governs ISO?

ISO is governed by a democratic system where national standards bodies from around the world collaborate.[citation:6]

General Assembly

Annual meeting of all members; ultimate authority for strategic decisions[citation:6]

ISO Council

Core governance body with 20 member bodies; meets three times yearly[citation:6]

Technical Management Board

Manages technical work and oversees technical committees[citation:6]

Technical Committees

Develop standards with input from experts worldwide[citation:2]

Key ISO Governance Principles

  • Global Relevance: Standards must be applicable worldwide, with regional differences noted only when necessary[citation:2]
  • Consensus-Based: Decisions require broad agreement among participating members[citation:2]
  • Performance-Based: Focus on outcomes rather than prescribed designs[citation:2]
